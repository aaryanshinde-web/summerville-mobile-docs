## Accounts: Account Settings & Beneficiaries

> The member-facing view of who owns and who inherits each membership — read-only today, with a call-to-action back to the credit union for changes that require a signature.

### Step-by-Step Workflow

#### Step 1: Membership Settings — Primary Members & Beneficiaries

The **Membership settings** tab shows the ownership type (Joint, Individual, Business) and lists the primary members on the membership. Below that, the Beneficiaries section lists any associated beneficiaries, or reads "There are no beneficiaries associated with this membership" when the field is empty — either way, changes here require a branch visit or secure message form, since beneficiary updates are a signed event.

### Summary

Account Settings is deliberately read-only on mobile for the ownership and beneficiary sections — these fields are the subject of frequent fraud attempts and regulatory expectations require a signed change request, so the app shows the member what's on file and routes them back through support to update it. The **Account settings** tab alongside Membership settings is where display preferences (primary account, nickname) are editable in-app.

### Key Use Cases

* Member double-checks beneficiary before a life event: read the Beneficiaries section, confirm it matches their estate plan.
* Joint ownership verification: confirm both joint members appear under Primary members before initiating a large transfer.
* Member wants to add a beneficiary: app shows empty list; route them to a branch or a secure message form from the Help menu.
