## Accounts: Request Account Closure

> Account closure is not a self-service tap-to-close flow. Navigate through Side Menu → More Options → Close Account to reach the policy page that routes you to the phone line.

### Step-by-Step Workflow

#### Step 1: Open the Side Menu

Tap the **☰** hamburger icon at the top-right of any screen.

![Step 1: Open the Side Menu](../.gitbook/assets/Screenshot_1776963761.png)

#### Step 2: Tap More Options

In the Side Menu, scroll to **More Options** and tap it.

![Step 2: Tap More Options](../.gitbook/assets/Screenshot_1776963761.png)

#### Step 3: Tap Close Account

On the More Options screen, six rows appear. Scroll to the bottom and tap **Close Account**.

![Step 3: Tap Close Account](../.gitbook/assets/Screenshot_1776964011.png)

#### Step 4: Read the Policy and Tap Call Us

The Request Account Closure screen opens with the routing text: *"If you need assistance closing an account or would like to delete your online access, please contact us by phone or secure message."* Two phone numbers are shown: **(541) 928-4536** and **(800) 950-4536**. A note block explains the data-retention policy. Tap **Call Us** to auto-dial from your phone, or **Back** to exit if you change your mind.

![Step 4: Read the Policy and Tap Call Us](../.gitbook/assets/Screenshot_1776964056.png)

### Summary

Closure is handled off-app by policy — the BSA/AML retention obligations and the irreversibility of the action make a live-agent conversation the right control, and this screen keeps you from ending up in the wrong self-service flow when your real intent is to close. The plain-language note about data retention heads off the common "but I asked you to delete me" conversation that can surface after the fact. The same screen also covers "delete my online access" — the two are handled by the same routing.

### Key Use Cases

* Member ready to close today: Side Menu → More Options → Close Account → tap **Call Us** to speed-dial the closure line.
* Member confused closure with unenrollment: **Back** returns to More Options; if the intent is just to remove digital access, the same call handles it.
* Member says they want all data deleted: the note on Step 4 explains retention requirements — members often realize they don't need to close after reading it.
