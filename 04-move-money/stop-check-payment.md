_Summerville Mobile › Move Money › Stop Check Payment_

## Move Money: Stop Check Payment

> Self-service stop-payment order on a paper check the member wrote — single check or a series, with the required Terms & Conditions acceptance before submit.

**How to get here:** Bottom navigation → **Deposit** → **Stop Check Payment** (also accessible from Side Menu → **Stop Check Payment**)

### Step-by-Step Workflow

#### Step 1: Enter Check Details and Accept Terms

From the Deposit tab → **Stop Check Payment** row, the form opens with the account pre-selected (e.g., **Retail Checking Account (#0001) — Available Balance $145,231.22**). Choose **Number of checks: Single check** or **Series of checks**. Enter the check number, amount, and optional memo. Tick the **"Please accept the Terms & Conditions"** checkbox to enable **Submit**; **Cancel** exits without saving.

### Summary

Stop Check Payment is a post-hoc control — it's only useful when the member already wrote the check and later realized it shouldn't clear. Because a stop-payment order has a fee and legal implications (the FI is committing to return the check if presented), the Terms & Conditions acceptance is a hard gate. The **Series of checks** option handles scenarios like a lost checkbook where multiple check numbers need to be stopped at once. Members should be reminded that stop-pay orders expire — typically 6 months — and must be renewed if the check hasn't been presented by then.

### Key Use Cases

* Member wrote a check to the wrong payee and hasn't mailed it yet: enter single check number, Submit, check can never clear.
* Lost or stolen checkbook: Series of checks, range of numbers, Submit; all checks in range rejected if presented.
* Member realizes after mailing they overpaid an invoice: stop-pay the original check, write a new one for the correct amount.
