# Skip A Pay

_Summerville Mobile › Move Money › Skip A Pay_

## Move Money: Skip A Pay

> Push a loan payment out by one cycle without calling the credit union. Pick the loan, accept the fee and terms, and the due date moves. Limited to twice in any 12-month period; a processing fee applies.

### Step-by-Step Workflow

#### Step 1: Open the Side Menu

Tap the **☰** hamburger icon at the top-right of any screen.

![Step 1: Open the Side Menu](../.gitbook/assets/Screenshot_1776963761.png)

#### Step 2: Scroll Down and Tap Skip A Pay

Scroll the Side Menu past the main navigation rows to the secondary area. Tap **Skip A Pay — Skip A Pay**.

![Step 2: Scroll Down and Tap Skip A Pay](../.gitbook/assets/Screenshot_1776963763.png)

#### Step 3: Select Which Loan to Skip

The **Skip A Pay** screen opens with three tabs at the top: **Skip A Payment** (active), **History**, **More**. Below the tabs, a loan selector shows your eligible loans: *Line of Credit (******9991L0006) — Due May 18, 2026 — Payment Due $300.00*; *Used Automobile (******9991L0004) — Due Apr 25, 2026 — Payment Due $450.00*. A **Disqualified Loans** section below lists loans that can't be skipped with the disqualification reason. Tap the loan you want to skip.

![Step 3: Select Which Loan to Skip](../.gitbook/assets/Screenshot_1776964170.png)

#### Step 4: Review the New Due Date and Fee

The confirm screen shows the current and new due dates side by side (e.g., **Current Due Date: May 18, 2026** → **Advanced Due Date: June 18, 2026**) plus the **Transaction Fee** ($35.00 in the capture). A note reminds you that *"You have the option to skip loan payment two times within a 12 month period."*

![Step 4: Review the New Due Date and Fee](../.gitbook/assets/Screenshot_1776964176.png)

#### Step 5: Pick Fee Payment Method and Accept Terms

Choose how to pay the $35 fee: **Credit Union Account** (with dropdown), **Charge my Credit Card**, or **Add to Loan Balance**. Read the **Terms & Conditions** block and tick *"I agree to the terms and fees as stated above"* to enable **Finish**. Tap **Finish** to submit.

![Step 5: Pick Fee Payment Method and Accept Terms](../.gitbook/assets/Screenshot_1776964185.png)

#### Step 6: Success Confirmation

On submit, a success screen appears with a large blue checkmark and the message *"Your request for a skip payment has been processed."* in green. Tap **Proceed** to return to the Skip A Pay home.

![Step 6: Success Confirmation](../.gitbook/assets/Screenshot_1776964183.png)

#### Step 7: View Skip History

Back on Skip A Pay, tap the **History** tab. Each request shows **Pending** or **Completed** status with the loan reference and *"Requested: date at time"*. The **More ▾** dropdown next to History includes **Notification History** for the related skip notifications.

![Step 7: View Skip History](../.gitbook/assets/Screenshot_1776964186.png)

### Summary

Skip A Pay is a one-screen-per-step flow that handles what used to be a phone call to the loan officer — you pick the loan, accept the fee, and the due date moves. The two-per-year limit is enforced by the system; loans that have hit the cap appear under **Disqualified** until the rolling 12-month window resets. The fee can come from checking, a credit card, or the loan balance itself. The History tab is your receipt for any past skip.

### Key Use Cases

* Unexpected expense eats cash you'd earmarked for the car loan: Skip A Pay → pick the auto loan → pay the $35 fee from checking → due date moves a month.
* Already skipped twice this year: loans show under Disqualified with "twice in a rolling 12 months" as the reason.
* Checking on a prior skip: History tab shows Pending / Completed per request with timestamps.
